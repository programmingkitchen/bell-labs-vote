The Heros of AT&T Bell Labs

This educational web app interactively teaches participants the history of computing. Focusing on the lives of the innovators, it examines several important milestones, all originating from Bell Labs roughly during the second half of the twentieth century.
While Bell Labs was responsible for many types of innovations, this app focuses on those relating to digital computing. Scientists at Bell Labs have been responsible for almost every important technical breakthrough over the last ninety years. From the innovations that are the backbone of modern digital computing to discovering the origins of the universe, researchers at Bell Labs did it.

This web app focuses on the specific innovations that represent the foundations of digital computing. Below you will find a thumbnail sketch of each significant computing-related innovation as well as some information about the innovators behind them. To learn more about these topics, visit:

Claude Shannon and Information Theory: In 1948, "A Mathematical Theory of Communication", one of the founding works in information theory, was published by Claude Shannon in the Bell System Technical Journal. It built in part on earlier work in the field by Bell researchers Harry Nyquist and Ralph Hartley, but it greatly extended these.
